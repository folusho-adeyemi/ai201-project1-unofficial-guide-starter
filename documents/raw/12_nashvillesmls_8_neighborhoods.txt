<p>Unravel the allure of <a href="/blog/best-neighborhoods-nashville.html">Nashville's best neighborhoods</a>, beckoning those looking to live near Vanderbilt University. Experience the fusion of modernity and charm amidst dining, shopping, and cultural hotspots. Uncover the essence of Nashville's history and vibrancy. Whether you want to live close to work, invest in kiddie condos near Vanderbilt University, or broaden your real estate portfolio, immerse yourself in the best Nashville neighborhoods near Vanderbilt University to find the perfect real estate opportunity for you.</p>
<h2 class="nmls-housing">Elliston Place</h2>
<p>Elliston Place is located in the heart of Nashville Midtown, just north of Vanderbilt University and across West End Avenue. It offers several bus routes along Church Street and Broadway that go around the university, though locals can reach the campus on foot in under 10 minutes. <strong>Some of Nashville's best live music venues, drinking holes, and eateries are located within walking distance of many Elliston Place homes.</strong> One popular spot is <a href="https://www.conservancyonline.com/flourishhere?gclid=CjwKCAjw-vmkBhBMEiwAlrMeFygvS6Lr8pReg7RF4CFTuJ-S8nzSO3KM8RTEwjwSS4OISXwHd0JMUBoChJ8QAvD_BwE" target="_blank">Centennial Park</a>, where residents can enjoy summer concerts, outdoor yoga, and more.</p>
<p><a href="/blog/did-you-know82-people-move-to-nashville-every-day.html">Living in Nashville</a> on the nightlife corridor along Elliston Place gives you access to long-standing music venues like EXIT/IN, Cross-Eyed Critters, Caf&eacute; Coco, and The End. Other Nashville staples located here include Elliston Place Soda Shop, Corner Bar, and I Love Sushi. Other eateries like Commodore Grille, Jason's Deli, and Michaelangelo's Pizza have become local favorites. In the neighborhood's northern section, locals can find top-notch medical facilities, like TriStar Centennial and Ascension Saint Thomas Hospital. For recreation nearby, Centennial Sportsplex offers swimming pools, tennis courts, a skating rink, and a fitness center.</p>
<p>Homes in Ellison Place tend to be part of Nashville Midtown's condo market, with many modern homes near Vanderbilt University dating back to the 2010s. <a title="Midtown Homes for Sale" href="/midtown.php">Home prices in Midtown</a> and Ellison Place range from $400,000 for a one-bedroom condo to $1.5 million for a large detached property.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">8th Ave S.</h2>
<p><strong>Known for its hip culture and independent businesses, Nashville's trendy 8th Ave S. neighborhood is situated about one mile east of Vanderbilt University.</strong> Locals can reach the campus in less than 10 minutes by biking or driving along Edgehill Avenue. Alternatively, it takes about 25 minutes to walk there or travel by bus via Wedgewood or Edgehill avenues. Locals are minutes away from I-65 and I-40, providing easy access to <a title="Downtown Nashville Homes for Sale" href="/downtown-real-estate.php">downtown Nashville</a> and other nearby areas.</p>
<p>The 8th Ave S. neighborhood offers a wide variety of local venues, shops, and eateries, with most of them located along the main thoroughfare. These include The Basement, an up-close music venue; 8th &amp; Roast, a craft coffee shop; and Zanies Comedy Club, a classic since 1983. Locals can also walk to hotspots like The Urban Juicer, Love Peace &amp; Pho, and Reservoir Park. The park offers athletic courts, walking paths, a playground, and a picnic pavilion. Other local amenities include Dogtopia Daycare, Rumble Seat Guitar Store, and a Publix Supermarket.</p>
<p><a href="/melrose-8th-ave-s.php">Homes for sale on 8th Ave S.</a> range from condos in established and newer buildings to single-household bungalows and new construction properties. Prices start at around $300,000 for a 600-square-foot condo and climb up to $2 million for a large <a title="Nashville Luxury Homes for Sale" href="/luxury-homes-estates.php">luxury property</a>.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">Edgehill</h2>
<img style="display: block; margin: 20px auto;" src="/uploads/agent-1/edgehill-village-community.jpg" alt="Living in the Edgehill Community" />
<p>Edgehill is situated just east of Vanderbilt University, and only a 10 minutes' walk to the campus from the neighborhood's core. It extends from Division Street in the north to Wedgewood Avenue in the south, with Vanderbilt University and 8th Avenue South marking its western and eastern boundaries, respectively. <strong>Edgehill features a quiet residential side with tree-lined streets and some of </strong><a href="/blog/best-parks-nashville.html"><strong>Nashville&rsquo;s best parks</strong></a>, as well as an urban commercial side that includes the popular Villa Place strip.</p>
<p>Around Villa Place, locals can find many of the neighborhood's gems, such as Barcelona Wine Bar, Jack Brown's Beer &amp; Burger Joint, and Sadie's Mediterranean Restaurant. Other notable places include Egghill for breakfast, Bella Napoli for Italian eats, and the cocktail lounge Old Glory, which is located in an underground boiler room. For outdoor recreation, Edgehill residents are within walking distance of some of the largest green spaces in Nashville Midtown, including Reservoir Park, Rosa Park, and Edgehill Community Garden. Shops and services like Aesop, Billy Reid, Moose Men's Grooming Lounge, and CrossFit Music City are also nearby.</p>
<p><a href="/edgehill-edgehill-village.php">Homes for sale in Edgehill</a> include traditional brick <a title="Nashville Ranch Homes for Sale" href="/nashville/ranch-homes.php">ranch homes</a>, modern condominiums, and new construction, single-household properties. Condos are generally priced between $400,000 and $800,000, while detached properties can range from $700,000 to $3 million.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">Hillsboro Village</h2>
<p><strong>Hillsboro Village developed in the late 1800s as a streetcar suburb of Nashville and is now considered a hip, modern neighborhood.</strong> Located one mile south of Vanderbilt University, it's just a 10-minute bike ride or a five-minute drive away from the campus. Locals appreciate the area's walkability, with many shops, restaurants, and entertainment options close by. The neighborhood is also home to Nashville landmarks like the Belcourt Theatre, Belcourt Mansion, and the Gallery of Iconic Guitars.</p>
<p>Hillsboro Village is known for its unique selection of eateries located on 21st Avenue South, including E3 Chophouse, Biscuit Love, and Fido. The Pancake Pantry is a local favorite, serving up stacks of its namesake breakfast item since 1961. Other spots worth checking out include The Grilled Cheeserie, Hopdoddy Burger Bar, and The Villager Tavern. Locals are also near one-of-a-kind boutiques like The Impeccable Pig, the Nash Collection, and Molly Green. Other local amenities include the Fisher Center for the Performing Arts, Bloom Flowers, and Cognito Hair Design.</p>
<p><a href="/hillsboro-village-real-estate.php">Real estate in Hillsboro Village</a> consists of early 1900s bungalows, <a title="Nashville Condos &amp; Lofts for Sale" href="/nashville-condos-lofts-townhomes.php">modern condos</a>, and new construction, single-household properties. Prices range from $500,000 for a two-bedroom condo to more than $3 million for a large luxury home.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">12 South</h2>
<p><strong>One of </strong><a href="/blog/nashvilles-most-walkable-neighborhoods.html"><strong>Nashville's most walkable neighborhoods</strong></a> is 12 South, which is situated southeast of Vanderbilt University. Locals can reach the campus in less than 10 minutes by car or bike. The neighborhood's main commercial street is <a title="12th South Avenue Commercial Real Estate for Sale" href="/nashville-12th-south-commercial.php">12th Avenue South</a>, running from Wedgewood Avenue in the north to the Four-Forty Parkway in the south. While the neighborhood's spine along 12th Avenue South is quite busy, the residential blocks surrounding it are quiet and leafy, providing a tranquil atmosphere for locals.</p>
<p>Dining options in 12 South range from MAFIAoZA's Pizzeria and Edly's Bar-B-Que to bartaco and Burger Up. Hip coffee shops like White Bison, Frothy Monkey, and Wanna Spoon Cereal Bar are popular gathering places, as are the classic watering holes Bottle Cap Bar, The Filling Station, and 12 South Taproom. Other neighborhood hotspots include Sevier Park, the 12th South Farmers Market, and White's Mercantile.</p>
<p><a title="12 South Homes for Sale" href="/12-south-real-estate.php">Homes in 12 South</a> include single-household properties from the early 1900s as well as newly built condos and townhomes. Architectural styles range from ranch-style homes and storybook <a title="Nashville Cottages for Sale" href="/nashville/cottage-homes.php">cottages</a> to classic <a title="Nashville Colonial Homes for Sale" href="/nashville/colonial-homes.php">colonials</a> and sleek <a title="Nashville Contemporary Homes for Sale" href="/nashville/contemporary-homes.php">contemporary designs</a>. Prices generally fall between $500,000 and $3 million.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">West End</h2>
<img style="display: block; margin: 20px auto;" src="/uploads/agent-1/west-end-community.jpg" alt="Living in the West End Neighborhood" />
<p><a href="/west-end-nashville.php">West End real estate</a> takes up a large portion of Nashville Midtown, running from I-40 in the north to Centennial Park's southwest corner, then curving to the east around Vanderbilt University. The campus is located in the neighborhood's southwestern corner and it's just a few minutes' stroll from the neighborhood's northern and central areas. <strong>Because of Vanderbilt's presence, West End features a plethora of eateries and bars near the campus, with several world-class venues right around the corner.</strong></p>
<p>Some of the best spots in West End include the down-home Indian eatery Bombay Palace, the fine-dining French establishment Chateau West, and the no-frills dive Springwater Bar. The neighborhood also offers plenty of shops, including the locally-owned outdoor gear store Cumberland Transit and the trendy boutique The French Shoppe. Additionally, locals are within walking distance of Vanderbilt University Medical Center, Centennial Park, and an array of <a href="https://nashvilletodo.com/must-visit-midtown-nashville-bars-for-a-fun-night/" target="_blank">live music venues and bars in Nashville Midtown.</a></p>
<p>Condos make up the bulk of West End's real estate market, but there are a few townhomes and detached properties located on the community's southern and eastern fringes. Prices range from $350,000 for a starter condo to upwards of $2 million for three or more bedrooms in a <a title="Nashville New Construction Homes for Sale" href="/nashville-new-construction.php">new construction property</a>.</p>

<h2 class="nmls-housing">Marathon Village</h2>
<p>Old brick factories and warehouses make up the Marathon Village district, with most buildings dating back to the early 1900s and repurposed for modern use. The neighborhood is located north of Vanderbilt University and is a five-minute drive or 10-minute bike ride away. Its centerpiece is the historic Marathon Motor Works building, a multi-level brick structure built in 1907. Today, it houses many trendy restaurants, bars, boutiques, and galleries.</p>
<p><strong>The neighborhood's open lots, colorful murals, and vintage architecture create an industrial-chic atmosphere that's unique to Nashville Midtown.</strong> Most of the action is centered along the Clinton Street corridor, where locals can find hotspots like Antique Archeology Nashville, Southern Engine Deli, and Marathon Music Works. While locals tend to venture out for groceries, there are a few local services and amenities within walking distance, including Third Coast Comedy Club, Corsair Distillery &amp; Taproom, and Barista Parlor.</p>
<p>Homes in Marathon Village are mostly single-household properties located south of the Marathon Motor Works building, with prices typically ranging from $400,000 to $700,000.</p>
<div class="hw-cutout" style="clear: both;">

<h2 class="nmls-housing">Melrose</h2>
<p><strong>With an array of local shops and services all within walking distance, Melrose offers residents easy access to a convenient urban lifestyle.</strong> The neighborhood is located southeast of Vanderbilt University, close to the I-65 and Four-Forty Parkway intersection. To reach the campus, locals can head north on Franklin Pike, then west on <a title="Wedgewood Houston Homes for Sale" href="/wedgewood-houston-area.php">Wedgewood Avenue</a>, and get there in 10 minutes by car or bike.</p>
