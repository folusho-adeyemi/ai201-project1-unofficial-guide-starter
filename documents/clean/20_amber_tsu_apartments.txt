About Tennessee State University

Tennessee State University (TSU), established in 1912 , is a prominent public historically black land-grant university located in Nashville, Tennessee . As the only state-funded historically black university in the state, TSU plays a vital role in providing accessible and high-quality education across diverse disciplines.

The university offers a broad array of programs, including 41 bachelor’s degrees , 23 master’s degrees, and 8 doctoral degrees . TSU is classified as a " Doctoral University with High Research Activity " by the Carnegie Classification, reflecting its robust research contributions. The campus spans 500 acres and houses over 65 buildings, making it the largest college campus in Nashville. TSU offers a range of on-campus housing options, including dormitories and apartment complexes, ensuring students have a comfortable living experience.

Student life at TSU is dynamic, with more than 60 registered student organizations, including the Student Government Association and the Aristocrat of Bands . Athletics play a key role at TSU, with the university competing in NCAA Division I as a member of the Ohio Valley Conference .

In 2024, TSU celebrated a milestone, surpassing $100 million in research funding , solidifying its position as a leader among historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs). Through its commitment to academic excellence, cutting-edge research, and community engagement, TSU remains a cornerstone of higher education in Tennessee and beyond.

Student Travel Near Tennessee State University

For students at Tennessee State University, navigating the vibrant city of Nashville is made convenient with a variety of public transportation options. Accessible and affordable, students can choose from buses, the metro system, and numerous cycling paths to explore the city or head to their classes at the Tennessee State University Nashville campus. Here we delve into the specific modes of transport that connect you to the heart of student life and Tennessee State University off-campus housing.

1. Buses

The local bus service operated by WeGo Public Transit offers extensive routes covering much of Nashville, including stops near Tennessee State University. Students can benefit from reduced fares at just $1.70 per ride, making it a highly cost-effective option. The buses run frequently, and students also have the option to purchase an all-access monthly pass for $44 , providing unlimited travel and ensuring you never miss a class.

2. Metro System

Although Nashville does not currently have a metro system, the WeGo Star commuter train connects the southeastern suburbs to downtown Nashville, with connecting bus routes to TSU. Single rides on the commuter train cost around $5.25 , with discounts available for students.

3. Cycling

With Nashville's initiative to become more bike-friendly, cycling is a great option for students. The city is expanding its network of bike lanes and paths, providing a healthy and economical travel choice. Another popular option is Nashville's B-cycle program, which allows users to rent bikes from automated kiosks across the city, making it perfect for quick trips around campus or the city.
